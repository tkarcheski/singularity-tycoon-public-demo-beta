# v0.7 — The Compute Layer (V2) (design spec)

Drafted 2026-06-28. Epic for issue #37. Sibling to the v0.6 **Network** epic (#15):
#15 owns the *network* layer (synergy fields, switches, floors, topology); this epic
owns the *compute/physical* layer (what you put ON the floor). Builds on v0.5
(allocation, RP, self-improvement) and v0.3 (wear/heat/entropy) — all modifiers stack.

## Problem

Compute today is two near-identical tiles (`gpu1`, `gpu2`) on one `compute` research
track. Every compute build is the same shape: more GPUs, more power, more cooling.
There's no reason to choose *what kind* of compute you run, and the AI's only fantasy
is "sell tokens / do research / self-improve." v0.7 turns compute into a layout-and-
specialization puzzle: distinct hardware archetypes with real roles, plus a **training**
lane where the AI improves its own inference economics using real-world techniques.

## Theme: the OSI spine

The game grows up the stack. v0.7 deepens **Layer 1 (Physical/compute)** and opens the
**Application** layer (training models). #15 fills in the **Network** layer between them.
Research tracks get reorganized under these layer headings (sub-issue F) so the tree
reads as "you are climbing the stack."

## A. Compute archetypes

Five compute roles, not five stat blocks. Each is a distinct decision:

| Tile | Role | Cost | Power | Cooling | Compute | Heat | Train× | Infer× | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CPU Orchestrator** | coordinator | 90 | −2 | −1 | 1 | 1 | 0 | 0.5 | from start (core) |
| **APU** | efficient/cheap | 80 | −2 | −1 | 4 | 1 | 0.6 | 0.8 | cheap early unlock |
| **GPU v1/v2** | workhorse (exists) | 120/400 | −4/−10 | −3/−8 | 6/22 | 3/8 | 1.0 | 1.0 | (v2 exists) |
| **TPU** | training specialist | 500 | −12 | −10 | 18 | 9 | 2.0 | 1.2 | RP unlock |
| **Quantum** | exotic spike | 2500 | −8 | −25 | 30 | 12 | 4.0 | 0.5 | cash+RP, **requires Cooling V (Cryo)** |

All numbers tunable in playtest. New per-tile fields in `TILE_TYPES`:
- `train` / `infer` — workload efficiency multipliers (see B).
- `feeds` (CPU only) — orchestration capacity (GPUs served before throttle).

### CPU Orchestration (folds in #23)
- Each CPU serves up to **4** adjacent/near GPUs (count-based first; upgrade to
  distance-weighted once #17 universal synergy lands).
- **Unfed GPUs throttle to 0.5× output.** Applied in `tick()` where GPU compute is
  summed, alongside the existing condition/cluster/brownout multipliers.
- Makes "feed the GPUs" a third axis next to power and cooling.

### Cluster bonus
- GPU adjacency bonus (existing `GPU_ADJ_BONUS`/`GPU_ADJ_HEAT`) generalizes to
  GPU+TPU clusters. APU and Quantum do **not** cluster (APU = standalone efficiency;
  Quantum = its own thing). Keep it simple; revisit under #17.

## B. Train vs Infer — the 4th allocation slider

`state.alloc` becomes `{ sell, research, self, train }` (normalized to 1).
The existing self-improve lane is the *risky* growth path (compounds output, feeds
entropy/singularity); **training is the safe growth path** (improves token economics,
no entropy cost).

Each tile splits its own compute by the global fractions, **weighted by its efficiency**
so archetypes matter under a global slider:
- Inference output (sellable tokens) `= alloc.sell × Σ(compute_i × infer×_i × …mults)`
- Training output `= alloc.train × Σ(compute_i × train×_i × …mults)`
- Research/self keep today's behavior (no per-tile weighting change).

Allocation UI gains a 4th slider (`train`).

**Payoff staging (so A+B ships without a dead currency):**
- **A+B (now):** training raises a **Model Efficiency** stat (`state.modelEff`, 1.0→cap)
  that multiplies inference token value — the immediate "training improves token
  value/efficiency" payoff. This is the direct, visible reward that makes the new
  archetypes worth playtesting.
- **C (next):** introduce spendable **Training Points (TP)** and the linear I→V
  optimization ladder; Model Efficiency becomes the sum of purchased techniques
  rather than an automatic curve.

## C. Optimization tech tree (spend TP)

A new research-style track, **Inference Optimization**, themed on real techniques.
**Decided: a linear I→V ladder** (consistent with the existing power/cooling/compute
tracks and their UI). Each tier costs TP and applies a cumulative token-economics
multiplier (the payoff Tyler wants: "training improves token value / efficiency"):

| Tier | Technique | Real-world effect | In-game effect (cumulative) |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | **Quantization** | lower precision → faster | +inference throughput (tokens per TFLOPS) |
| II | **Pruning** | remove weights → smaller | further throughput + slight power/cooling relief |
| III | **Distillation** | small models, more accuracy | bigger token multiplier; extra boost to APU output |
| IV | **Speculative decoding** | predict-ahead shortcuts | "shortcut" token-output multiplier |
| V | **Sparsity** | skip zeros → shorter path | top-tier effective output multiplier |

Costs (in TP) scale steeply so the ladder is a long-game sink. Mirrors the
`RESEARCH` tier structure exactly — same `out` cumulative-multiplier pattern, paid
in TP instead of RP.

## D. Cooling tile variety

Today cooling depth is only the `RESEARCH.cooling` tiers; the only cooling *tiles* are
`fan` and `cooler`. Add tile variety that plugs into `computeHeatMap()`:
- **Heat Exchanger** — wider range, lower per-tile drain (covers a spread-out farm).
- **Immersion Tank** — huge drain, tiny range (drop-in for a hot cluster), high upkeep.
- (Quantum's **Cryo** dependency is satisfied by Cooling research tier V, not a tile.)
New tiles declare their drain profile the same way `COOLER_DRAIN`/`FAN_DRAIN` do.

## E. Robot ↔ Human proximity friction

New social-physics rule from the #37 dump:
- Humans (`human` pods) may sit **anywhere** — no compute-proximity requirement for them.
- **Bots (`botbay`) too close to human pods** apply a penalty (e.g. human skill-gain or
  sentiment −X within range 1). Models "give people space from the robots."
- **Researchable away over time** — a tier that shrinks/removes the penalty. Ties to
  #34 (Bot Bay upgrade path). Distinct from #34's repair-rate buff; can share a track.

## Decisions (locked 2026-06-28)

1. **Archetype numbers:** build with the spec defaults; tune during playtest.
2. **APU:** cheap early unlock (not a minute-zero tile).
3. **Optimization tree:** linear I→V ladder, paid in TP, mirroring `RESEARCH`.
4. **CPU orchestration:** **throttle framing** (faithful to #23). GPUs and TPUs run at
   **0.5×** output unless a CPU feeds them (each CPU feeds up to `feeds` accelerators).
   This *intentionally* retrofits existing CPU-less saves — call it out in the help
   modal / ticker so returning players know to add a CPU. APU and Quantum are
   self-contained (not subject to orchestration). Because the CPU is now mandatory
   for GPUs, it is a **core, ungated starter tile** (not an unlock); APU/TPU/Quantum
   remain unlocks.
